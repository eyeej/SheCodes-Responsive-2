Why Explore?

We believe words are more than definitions — they are sparks of curiosity, bridges of understanding, and tools for expression. This dictionary is not just a place to look up a word, but a space to explore, connect, and apply language in meaningful ways. Beyond quick searches, we aim to create an environment where words intertwine, ideas grow, and curiosity is continually satiated.

Libraries

Locations to study in Sydney

State Library of New South Wales

The State Library of New South Wales, part of which is known as the Mitchell Library, is a large heritage-listed special collections, reference and research library open to the public and is one of the oldest libraries in Australia.

Address:

Mitchell Library, 1 Shakespeare Pl, Sydney NSW 2000

What I like about it

A welcoming and central location for those who want to learn and explore.

Learn More

Darling Square Library

Located across two levels of The Exchange, this hub offers facilities designed to support learning, creativity, and innovation. It includes a fully equipped makerspace, a dedicated children’s area, and versatile spaces for reading, study, and work.

Address:

The Exchange, levels 1 and 2 1 Little Pier Street Haymarket 2000

What I like about it

Accessbility and atmosphere.

Learn More

Customs House Library

Situated in the heart of Circular Quay, this contemporary library spans two levels of Customs House. It provides welcoming spaces to read, research, connect with others, or simply relax.

Address:

31 Alfred Street Circular Quay 2000

What I like about it

A standout feature of the Customs House Library is its setting within the historic Customs House building, right on Circular Quay. The contrast of a modern, contemporary library inside a landmark heritage building makes it unique.
